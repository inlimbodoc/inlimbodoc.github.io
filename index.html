About

In Limbo focuses on a 24-year old Vancouver woman from an affluent family who lives with depression, bipolar disorder, and severe anxiety. She is the one in five people who experience significant mental health problems in B.C.

Living with depression and bipolar disorder is very difficult, Ashley has had to deal with depression her whole life and when it gets too difficult, her only option is a trip to the ER to prevent her from hurting herself.

However Ashley explains she’s often felt in limbo when it comes to her health, as she explains she is not “sick enough” to be considered a danger to herself or others; yet, she is not healthy enough to be able to lead a “normal” life and finish school or hold a steady job.

Her journey has taken her into the world of drugs, where she sometimes finds peace. Despite having experience first-hand the risks of consuming drugs, she would rather find happiness while high that sometimes having to deal with reality and her depression.

Mental health has been an issue on the rise across Canada. Recent statistics suggest one in five Canadians suffer from mental health issues. The cost of ignoring mental health problems affects everyone; according to a report B.C. spent over $1.3 billion dollars on mental health. St. Paul’s hospital alone has seen an increase of over 43% of hospital visits related to mental issues.

Through this documentary we are hoping to find out if the government is doing enough to help those individuals with these invisible yet debilitating diseases.

Producers

Estefania Duran

After obtaining her B.A. in Political Science, Estefania Duran went on to study at the London School of journalism. Her experience at LSJ and her passion to approach information in a critical manner, led her to the Broadcast and Online journalism Program at BCIT. When she is not running around looking for her next story, you can find Estefania on her yoga mat or baking her stress away.

Olivia Smith

Olivia went to Simon Fraser University for four years where she studied Communications, Art, and Technology. The classes that interested her the most were her journalism and TV/Film classes, prompting her to attend the Broadcast Journalism program at BCIT. Since then, Olivia has found a great passion in making television. She began interning at Vancouver-based Lark Productions where she learned the ins and outs of reality TV development. But when an opportunity came for her to work in the field for Lark’s own Emergency Room: Life + Death at VGH, she knew she had a found her true calling. She hopes to one day produce award-winning shows in either unscripted or scripted programming but realizes she still has a lot to learn. Either way, she is ready to take on any challenge that comes her way so long as she gets to be creative and inquisitive.
